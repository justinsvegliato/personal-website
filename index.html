Hi, I'm Justin.

I'm a first year grad student at UMass Amherst working in the Resource-Bounded Reasoning Group under Dr. Shlomo Zilberstein. My research focuses mainly on problems in automated reasoning. I currently work on planning algorithms that enable semi-autonomous systems to keep humans in the loop by smoothly transferring control to them during difficult tasks. I'm interested in philosophy, especially free will, mind, metaphysics, and epistemology as well.

Research

Distributed Graph Databases

I worked in the G* Research Group under Dr. Alan Labouseur on distributed graph databases. We investigated how to best place vertices across workers in distributed graph databases to speed up queries like PageRank and average degree.

  • Labouseur, A.G., Svegliato, J., & Hwang, J. (Working Paper). Distributed Graph Snapshot Placement and Query Performance in a Data Center Environment.

Stimulus-Response Testing

Dr. Carolyn Matheus and I built OpenSR, an open source stimulus-response testing framework. We wanted to create a free, easy-to-use alternative to expensive products already on the market.

  • Matheus, C.C. & Svegliato, J. (November, 2015). OpenSR: An open source stimulus-response framework. Human Technology.
  • Matheus, C.C. & Svegliato, J. (October, 2013). Open Stimulus-Response Framework. Presented at Marist College Research Symposia, Poughkeepsie, New York.
  • Matheus, C.C. & Svegliato, J. (May, 2013). Stimulus-Response Tests: An Applied Demonstration. In Proceedings of IEEE International Conference on Research Challenges in Information Science, Paris, France. DOI: 10.1109/RCIS.2013.6577740

Philosophy

Outside of computer science, I've worked on problems in free will, agency, metaphysics, epistemology, and mind. I published a definition of knowledge based on ordinary differential equations and presented on why we can't have free will at a conference.

  • Svegliato, J. (April, 2014). The Inadequate Ontological Foundation of Libertarian Free Will. Presented at the 4th Mid-Hudson Undergraduate Philosophy Conference, Poughkeepsie, New York.
  • Svegliato, J. (January, 2013). The Revival of Reliabilism: A Mathematical Approach to the Generality Problem. Marist Undergraduate Philosophy Journal, 1, 18-33.
